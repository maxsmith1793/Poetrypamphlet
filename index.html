Welcome to "A Poetry Pamphlet":

Our efforts are an attempt to delightfully display poetic form in a manner that is nonaggressive and that refrains from indulging in excessive darkness. We try to be realistic with a gentle attitude and touch, which naturally inspires artistic form. Please enjoy each edition.

“An Ironic World” by Max Smith

I find it strangely amusing,
How we always play so many games,
Often, I think I am like the king of this world,
With a golden throne in outer space,
I could boss around my vassals,
Tell my servants what to do,
But then I realize,
I am still sitting in my desk chair,
I am still in this small room!

Sometimes I entertain,
this petty pond of ‘peace,'
I’m similar to an arrogant frog,
I croak, inflated, on my "enormous" lily pad,
I hop and I puff myself up peculiarly,
Yet, a nearby bystander mocks me laughingly,
To them, I am just a little frog,
Why so dreadfully serious?
“The Ocean” by Kali Smith

Somersaulting swimmers in its wakeful force,
The ocean loves to show me the feeling of remorse,
It reminds me through its fluctuating tide,
that I am only a spectator coming along for the ride,
When I float among the waves created by the moon,
I become a part of the ocean too,
The ocean unfailingly reminds me,
That I am oh so tiny.
“The Strangeness of it All” by Max Smith

Happiness - a mysterious creature,
Grasping it is like trying to throw a net around a unicorn,
Right when I think I've wrangled the majestic stallion,
I look to simply see that my capture has magically vanished!
Happiness is like holding sand between one’s fingertips,
Despite my effort to keep it in my hands, it all dissolves,
Back to the universe once again.
